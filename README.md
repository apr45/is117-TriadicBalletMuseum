# Museum Site Concept: The Triadic Ballet

An exploration of the plotless experimental dance piece by German Bauhaus artist **Oskar Schlemmer**. The project is structured around the concept of "trinity," featuring a performance broken into three distinct acts, centered around three color themes and geometric costumes that restricted the dancers' movements.

---

## 🎨 Design Style
The aesthetic captures the **minimalist, geometric, and functional** principles of the Bauhaus movement. The visual identity leans heavily into the "threeness" of the ballet, utilizing the primary color palette of the original production:
* **Yellow**
* **Pink**
* **Black**

---

## 🧠 Cialdini Principles
* **Authority:** Establishes credibility by researching and verifying the historical context of each piece of information.
* **Unity:** Fosters a shared identity with the audience by framing the website as a **collaborative avant-garde journey**.
* **Commitment & Consistency:** Incorporates small user interactions and external links to related the Triadic Ballet to drive deeper exploration.

---

## 🎭 Brand Archetype
* **The Sage:** Presents research-based, verified details regarding the origins and legacy of the ballet for an audience interested in the avant-garde.
* **The Creator:** Captures the original expression of the Bauhaus and the Triadic, implementing an imaginative and artistic theme rather than a traditional, sterile museum approach.

---

## ⚙️ Agentic Orchestration Process

1.  **Ideation:** Listed concepts, goals, style, and tools in a Google Doc.
2.  **Consultation:** Used **Gemini** to provide feedback on the document and generate a file structure for the GitHub repository.
3.  **Guidelines:** Developed a comprehensive guide containing instructions for page structure, design rules, constraints, vocabulary, and verification management.
4.  **Sprints:** Divided the project into three manageable sprints.
5.  **Documentation & Verification:**
    * Maintained a Markdown file of essential prompts.
    * Verified all AI-generated research in a separate validation file.
6.  **Iterative Development:** Generated full HTML/CSS for pages, then broke them into smaller components to tweak and refine according to the vision.
