01 / ORIGIN

THE
TRIADIC
BALLET

An avant-garde dance piece designed and choreographed by the Bauhaus artist Oskar Schlemmer. Transcending traditional performances, Das Triadische Ballett transforms the human form into a medium to explore the relationship between the body and space.

It stripped off all ornamental amenities associated with traditional ballet, prioritizing geometric and restrictive components. The production, first premiered in 1922 at the Württemberg Landestheater in Stuttgart, combines dance, costume, music, and set design into the ultimate Gesamtkunstwerk.

While the original 1922 film has not survived, the ballet left behind a legacy of modernist, bizarre performance art.
